# Stride stretching plan — ~20-min evening, stiff beginner

For a very stiff beginner; daily ~20-min evening follow-along, rotated occasionally. The
planner surfaces ONE video at a time over weeks. Evidence + curated videos + durability.

## Evidence
- **Static stretching wins for gaining ROM** (vs dynamic, which is for warm-ups). Konrad 2024 meta-analysis.
- **Hold ~60s total per stretch** (e.g. 3×20s); **low intensity ≈ high intensity** for gains — stretch to mild tension, **never pain** (Harvard Health).
- **Frequency:** 2–3×/wk minimum; **daily is fine and faster**. Evening is good (muscles warm; winds you down).
- **Timeline:** feel looser ~2–3 wks; measurable ROM ~4–8 wks; big milestones (toes) ~3–6 months. Consistency > intensity.

## Rotation
- **Wk 1–4:** Video 1 nightly (memorise, build streak); Video 6 on tired nights.
- **Wk 5–8:** alternate 2 and 4 (same difficulty, new movements).
- **Wk 9–12:** add 5 (bedtime) 2–3 nights; 7 on tight-hip nights.
- **Graduation (when easy):** 8, then a ~30-min intermediate flow from the same channels.
Rule throughout: mild tension, ~30–60s/stretch, never pain.

## Durability (how the app should reference videos)
Don't trust single IDs alone. **Best: an app-owned YouTube playlist** + per-item store `{id, title, channel}`; if an ID 404s, **fall back to a YouTube search link** (`results?search_query=title+channel`). Every pick is on a large long-lived channel as the safety net. "Verify on first surface" flag for the med-confidence ones (3, 5, 7).
